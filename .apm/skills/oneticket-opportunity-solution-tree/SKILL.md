## Purpose
Guide through creating an Opportunity Solution Tree (OST): extract target outcomes, generate opportunities (problems to solve), map solutions, and select the best proof-of-concept based on feasibility, impact, and market fit.

## Structure

```
Desired Outcome
      |
  Opportunity 1 → Solutions → Experiments
  Opportunity 2 → Solutions → Experiments
  Opportunity 3 → Solutions → Experiments
```

## Process

1. **Extract desired outcome** — measurable business/product metric
2. **Identify 3 opportunities** — customer problems driving the outcome
3. **Generate 3 solutions per opportunity**
4. **Evaluate solutions** — score on feasibility, impact, market fit
5. **Select POC** — best starting point with testable hypothesis
6. **Define experiment** — A/B test, prototype, or concierge test

## Rules

- Opportunities are customer problems, not solutions in disguise
- Always diverge before converging — minimum 3 opportunities, 3 solutions each
- Outcomes must be measurable
- Every solution must map to an experiment
- Limit to 3 opportunities and 9 solutions to avoid analysis paralysis
